Reading: Emotional Intelligence (EQ)

We all know about IQ (Intelligence Quotient), which measures how smart you are academically. But have you heard of EQ? Emotional Intelligence is the ability to understand and manage your own emotions, as well as recognize emotions in others.


People with high EQ are usually very empathetic. This means they can put themselves in someone else's shoes. For example, if a colleague is quiet, a high-EQ person might ask if they are okay, rather than assuming they are rude. In the workplace, EQ is becoming just as important as technical skills because it helps with teamwork and leadership.


Fortunately, unlike IQ which is hard to change, you can improve your EQ with practice. It starts with self-awareness: stopping to ask yourself, "Why am I feeling this way?"
