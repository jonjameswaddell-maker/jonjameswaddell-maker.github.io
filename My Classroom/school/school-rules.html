Reading: The Strict Academy

Welcome to Blackwood Academy, one of the most prestigious schools in the country. However, academic excellence comes at a price: strict discipline. Students must be punctual; the school gates lock at 8:00 AM sharp. If you are late, you receive immediate detention after school.


The uniform policy is rigorous. Blazers must be worn at all times, even in summer. Cheating on exams results in immediate suspension, and bullying is met with a zero-tolerance policy. Despite these harsh rules, the principal believes that discipline creates success. "We teach respect first, mathematics second," he often says during the morning assembly.

Cultural Corner: Rules Around the World

School rules vary wildly depending on where you live. In Japan, students often have to clean their own classrooms. This is called 'O-soji'. It teaches them respect for their environment. They also cannot wear makeup or jewelry.


In the USA, the dress code is usually more relaxed in public schools, though private schools often require uniforms. However, "Hall Passes" are common‚Äîstudents cannot walk in the corridor without a paper pass from a teacher.


In Finland, the rules are very relaxed. Students address teachers by their first names, there are very few exams, and homework is minimal. They believe play and rest are crucial for learning.
