Reading: The Digital Detox

In a world dominated by screens, many people are returning to "analog" hobbies. Activities like knitting, pottery, and gardening are becoming popular again. These are often called "Digital Detox" hobbies because they force you to put down your phone.


Psychologists say that working with your hands reduces stress. When you accomplish something tangible, like baking a loaf of bread or painting a canvas, your brain releases dopamine. Unlike video games, which provide instant gratification, these pastimes require patience and dedication. Becoming an amateur photographer or carpenter gives people a sense of control in a chaotic world.
